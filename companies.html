How Companies Handle Data

Google

  • Collects vast amounts of user data across its services (Search, Gmail, Maps, YouTube, etc.).
  • Uses data for targeted advertising, product improvement, and AI training.
  • Offers privacy controls, but default settings often favor data collection.
  • Has faced scrutiny and fines for data privacy violations in the EU and elsewhere.
  • Stores data in global data centers with advanced security, but is subject to government requests.

Microsoft

  • Emphasizes enterprise security and compliance, especially for business/cloud customers.
  • Collects telemetry and diagnostic data from Windows and Office products.
  • Provides granular privacy settings, but some data collection is required for functionality.
  • Invests heavily in cybersecurity and transparency reports.
  • Has responded to privacy concerns by increasing user control and data export options.
