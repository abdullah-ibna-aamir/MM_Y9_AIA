How Companies Handle Data

Google

  • Collects lots of user data across its services (Search, Gmail, Maps, YouTube, etc.).
  • Uses data for ads, product improvement, and AI training.
  • Privacy controls exist, but default settings usually mean more data collection.
  • Has faced fines for privacy violations.
  • Stores data in global data centers with strong security, but is subject to government requests.

Microsoft

  • Focuses on security and compliance for business/cloud customers.
  • Collects diagnostic data from Windows and Office.
  • Privacy settings are available, but some data collection is required.
  • Invests in cybersecurity and transparency.
  • Offers more user control and data export options.
